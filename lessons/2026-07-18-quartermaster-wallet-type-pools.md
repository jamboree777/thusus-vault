# Wallet-type capital pools — separate what's locked from what can move

The Quartermaster's job is to decide *how much working capital each venue should hold and how to move it there cheapest*. Strategy #4 (Covered Arb) breaks the assumption that a venue has **one** pool: it locks collateral for a margin short and margin for a perp hedge, and neither of those dollars is transferable working capital any more. A single-pool model quietly **double-counts** them — the floor planner would happily fund a spot deficit with dollars that are actually pinned behind a hedge.

v0.2 gives the plan a **wallet-type** vocabulary: `working_capital` (transferable), `hedge_uta` / `hedge_futures` / `hedge_margin` (locked), and `lending_collateral` (locked + **external**). Every hedge reservation is subtracted from the venue's available working capital **at the venue where the money is actually drawn**, so there is exactly one claim on each dollar.

## Where the money is drawn is the whole trick

- **Gate ($75 futures float)** is a *classic* account: spot and futures are separate wallets, so the float is seated by an **internal spot→futures wallet_move** — instant, free, no chain. Gate has $1,049 spot, so it self-funds; charge gate $75.
- **Bybit ($300 UTA)** is a *unified* account: one pool serves margin-short collateral **and** perp margin, so no internal move is ever needed — but bybit holds **$0** in the paper fund. To seat $300 there, working capital must **move venues**. The plan sources it from the largest working-capital venue (binance) via a cross-venue USDT transfer, and charges **binance** the $300, not bybit. That is the "no double counting" rule doing real work: the locked dollars leave binance's spendable pool, exactly once.
- **Bithumb (₩7.5M lending collateral)** is **external** — Robin's additional money, not carved from the $10k. It is a pool with `funded=false`, `source=external`, and it never touches the working-capital floor/cap until the real deposit lands.

## Verify the product, then verify the route

Two verifications gate the plan, both against **live public APIs**, both stored in the plan:

- **Product** — do the instruments exist *right now*? bybit **743** linear perps trading and **263** spot symbols flagged UTA-margin-enabled (`marginTrading`); gate **835** futures contracts and **735** margin pairs. UTA itself is an account-level setting we can't read without auth, so it is marked `verified_via='docs+instruments'` — honest about the boundary rather than pretending. A venue that fails to verify is **not** reserved (the aux pool falls back to binance futures, logged loudly).
- **DW route** — for every inter-venue move, is USDT withdraw open at the source **and** deposit open at the destination on a shared chain? The binance→bybit funding move verified on **bsc**. The bithumb inflow is a **fiat KRW wire** (not a crypto route → `route_verified=null`), with a USDT rail (binance→bithumb via kaia) noted as the alternate if collateral is ever brought in as stablecoin.

## Still shadow

No keys, no execution — this is the Planner producing a *plan*. The value is that the plan is now honest about locked vs movable capital, carries a concrete seating route for each hedge pool, and refuses to reserve against an unverified product or an unverified transfer route. The reservation **sizes** ($300 / $75 / ₩7.5M) are seed values from the front plan; they get tuned from the hedged engine's measured concurrent-hedge pace.
