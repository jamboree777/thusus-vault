# 2026-07-15 — Thusus fund reset (ruleset finalization + full wipe)

On 2026-07-15, [[Thusus]]'s paper-trading book was **fully wiped and re-epoched** after the trading ruleset was finalized. This note records the reset and, more importantly, *why* a reset is an honesty feature rather than a cover-up.

## What happened (verified)

- **Ruleset finalized.** The execution gates that define the machine — [[executable-spread]] pricing, [[transfer-feasibility]] checks, [[quiet-size]] footprint limits, the [[repeat-haircut]], and the [[five-min-settlement]] accounting — were locked into their current form.
- **Full wipe.** All prior paper history was cleared. The book does **not** carry forward trades taken under earlier, different rules.
- **New fund epoch: 2026-07-15 07:09 UTC.** Two paper pools were established:
  - a **$3,000 KRW-side pool** (for [[kimchi-premium]] / [[won-carry]] strategies), and
  - a **$7,000 USDT-side pool** (for global [[one-price]] arb).
- **Still PAPER.** No real capital. Fills are simulated against real order-book quotes under the [[five-min-settlement]] model.

## Why a reset preserves honesty

It is tempting to read "they wiped the track record" as hiding something. The opposite is true. A track record accumulated across **changing rules** is meaningless — worse, it is an invitation to cherry-pick: keep the good runs from the old ruleset, discard the bad, and present a flattering blend that no single consistent strategy ever produced.

A clean epoch forbids that. From 07:09 UTC on 2026-07-15, every settled trade in the [[journal/2026-07-15|journal]] is generated by **one fixed ruleset**, so the P&L, win rate, and — most tellingly — the [[expectation-gap]] all describe the *same* machine. There is no survivorship blend and no pre-history to lean on. The number is small and the sample is young precisely because it is honest.

The first day under the new epoch is recorded in [[journal/2026-07-15]]: 52 settled trades, +$30.20 realized against $100.96 expected, an 80.8% win rate, and a large, instructive [[expectation-gap]]. That is what one ruleset, honestly accounted, actually looks like on day one — and it is the only kind of record worth compounding.
