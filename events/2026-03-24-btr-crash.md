# 2026-03-24 — BTR / Bitlayer crash and the stranded premium

This is the flagship case of a [[mirage-arb]]: a real, persistent cross-exchange premium on [[BTR]] at [[bitget]] that cannot be captured, because the market behind it is being quietly wound down. Every line below is graded — plain prose for what NightWatch can verify on exchange APIs and official docs, and an explicit `[media-reported, unverified]` tag for anything from the press.

## What NightWatch verifies (authoritative sources)

- **Identity.** BTR on gateio, bitget, kucoin, and mexc is the official **Bitlayer** token, verified against Bitlayer's own documentation across three chains: BSC `0xfed13d0c40790220fbde712987079eda1ed75c51`, Ethereum `0x6c76de483f1752ac8473e2b4983a873991e70da7`, Bitlayer chain `0x0e4cf4affdb72b39ea91fa726d291781cbd020bf`. There is an **unrelated** "Bitrue Coin (BTR)" on Ethereum at a different contract — a [[identity-verification|symbol collision]] to exclude, not part of this token.
- **Bitget deposit closed.** Bitget lists BTR on Ethereum only, and its Ethereum **deposit is closed** — Bitget's own public API returns `rechargeable = false` (confirmed 2026-07-12). Withdrawals stayed open: a **one-way door**.
- **Duration.** Bitget closed BTR deposits in **March 2026** citing "wallet maintenance," and they remained closed into mid-July 2026 — roughly **four months**. Routine wallet maintenance lasts hours to days; four months is not maintenance.
- **Perp removed.** Bitget's `BTRUSDT` perpetual has been **removed** — its mix API returns *"the symbol has been removed"* (code 40309); no BTR appears in USDT/COIN/USDC futures. So the rich Bitget market offers **no spot delivery in** (deposit closed) and **no synthetic short** (perp delisted).
- **Transfer matrix.** mexc (hub, all three chains open) ↔ gateio ↔ kucoin remain movable among themselves directly or via the [[mexc]] hub. **Every route into bitget deposit is blocked** — because of the one Ethereum-deposit closure, *not* because it is a different token.

## NightWatch's reading

Line the verified facts against the reported story and the sequence tells itself: a token blows up on leverage → the exchange **pulls the perpetual** (you don't keep a derivatives market open on something that just liquidation-cascaded) → and **freezes deposits while leaving withdrawals open** (stop new money flowing in; let stranded holders leave). The "premium" left behind is therefore almost certainly a **stale ghost quote of a market being wound down**, not real demand.

**Verdict: don't chase the premium.** The gap is real; the trade is not. This is the canonical lesson behind [[mirage-arb]] and [[transfer-feasibility]]. See also [[bitget]] and [[BTR]].
